Origin Protocol — Three December 2025 In-Scope Strategies
Target files: MorphoV2Strategy.sol, BaseCurveAMOStrategy.sol, CurveAMOStrategy.sol

These three contracts were added to the Immunefi scope on 30 December 2025.
They are less audited than the core Vault contracts and interact with VaultCore
in ways that may not have been fully stress-tested.

ARCHITECTURE:
All three strategies sit between VaultCore and external DeFi protocols:
- MorphoV2Strategy: deposits assets into Morpho V2 lending markets to earn yield.
  Interacts with Morpho's ERC4626-compatible vault interface.
- BaseCurveAMOStrategy: base contract for Curve AMO (Algorithmic Market Operations).
  Mints/burns OTokens via VaultCore.mintForStrategy/burnForStrategy to provide
  liquidity to Curve pools. This is the most complex — it has TWO-WAY interaction
  with VaultCore, not just one-way deposits.
- CurveAMOStrategy: extends BaseCurveAMOStrategy for specific Curve pool deployment.

CRITICAL DISTINCTION FROM NORMAL STRATEGIES:
Normal strategies (Aave, Morpho) only receive assets from the Vault and deposit
them externally. AMO strategies additionally call back into the Vault:
  - mintForStrategy(amount): mints OTokens without backing asset deposit
  - burnForStrategy(amount): burns OTokens without asset withdrawal
These functions are NOT protected by nonReentrant in VaultCore because AMO
strategies are expected to call them. This is an explicitly documented assumption.

KEY INTERFACES TO INVESTIGATE:
- How does each strategy report its balance back to VaultCore (_checkBalance)?
- What happens if a strategy's reported balance diverges from reality?
- What are the preconditions for depositAll(), withdrawAll(), withdraw()?
- Who can call harvest() on each strategy and what does it do with proceeds?
- What external protocols does each strategy trust implicitly?

HARD EXCLUSIONS (same as Vault run):
- No hypotheses requiring Governor/Strategist/Operator to act maliciously.
- No MEV/ordering attacks.
- No oracle operator compromise.
- Every hypothesis must be executable by an arbitrary unprivileged address.

KNOWN ISSUES (already confirmed excluded):
- The mint/burn AMO reentrancy path via defaultStrategy misconfiguration
  (hypothesis 4.1 from the Vault run) — this requires governance misconfiguration.
- maxSupplyDiff == 0 finding — killed by live on-chain state (3% on OETH, 5% on OUSD).
- rebaseOptIn yield front-running — killed by _rebaseOptIn require guard.

WHAT TO LOOK FOR SPECIFICALLY:
The most valuable surface here is the AMO strategy's two-way VaultCore interaction.
Focus on:
1. Can a permissionless actor manipulate the Curve pool price/balance in a way that
   causes mintForStrategy or burnForStrategy to mint/burn the wrong amount?
2. Does _checkBalance in the AMO strategy correctly account for ALL assets
   (both in the Curve pool and any held directly), especially at edge states?
3. Is there a state where the AMO strategy's reported balance to VaultCore
   diverges from its actual claimable balance, enabling over-minting or
   under-backing of OTokens?
4. What happens during withdrawAll() if the Curve pool has insufficient liquidity?
   Can this permanently strand assets or prevent the vault from serving redemptions?
5. For MorphoV2Strategy specifically: does it correctly handle the case where
   Morpho's reported assets differ from actual claimable assets (e.g. during
   bad debt socialization in Morpho markets)?
